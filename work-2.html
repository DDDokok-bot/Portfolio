Overview

This project explores how a simple environmental control can reshape the mood of an entire scene. By dragging the sun, the user can transition the sky between daytime and nighttime, turning a single interaction into a broader change in atmosphere.

Once the scene shifts into night, additional interactions appear: clicking creates fireworks, while hovering over stars causes them to fall diagonally like shooting stars. The project focuses on playful transitions, layered interaction, and making the environment feel responsive to user input.

What I Designed

Time Shift Interaction

Designed the main drag interaction so moving the sun feels like directly controlling the transition between morning, evening, and night.

Layered Night Events

Added secondary interactions that only appear at night, giving the project a stronger sense of reward, discovery, and progression.

Atmospheric Motion

Focused on timing, visual rhythm, and motion response to make the sky feel lively rather than static, while keeping the overall interaction simple and readable.

Process

The project started from the idea of making a sky feel interactive through one clear gesture. Instead of building many unrelated controls, I used the sun as the core input and let that movement influence the entire environment.

From there, I developed the nighttime interactions as a second layer, so the scene would feel deeper after the initial day-to-night transition. This helped create a small but engaging sequence: shift the sky, discover new behaviors, and continue exploring through motion and play.
