Overview

This project explores how a user moves between different spatial layers, perspectives, and levels of detail. The main view begins as a map-like environment containing several personally important places, including a classroom, the road to school, a commercial street I often walk through, and an archery range connected to my hobby.

Each location is marked by a green glowing circular effect that represents its “sound.” From the map view, users can hover over these signals to activate sound-based feedback and then click to transition into a more detailed scene. The project focuses on how interaction, sound, and rendering style can shape the way a place is perceived.

What I Designed

Layer Transition

Designed the movement from a large map-like overview into more detailed location scenes, making the transition between spatial layers feel intentional and readable.

Sound-Based Interaction

Used green glowing circular signals to represent places through sound. Hover changes both sound intensity and the wave effect based on the cursor’s distance from the center.

Detail vs. Distance

Built detailed location scenes with Gaussian splatting so nearby space appears with richer color and structure, while distant space remains as minimal flickering white particles.

Interaction Logic

In the main map view, each important location is highlighted by a green circular effect that continuously expands outward from its center and fades away. When the user hovers over one of these signals, the visual behavior changes from repeating expansion to a fixed circle whose wave amplitude responds to cursor distance.

Hovering closer to the center increases both the sound intensity and the visibility of the wave pattern. When the cursor leaves, the sound disappears. Clicking on a signal moves the main camera toward that location and then transitions the user into the corresponding detailed scene.

Scene Experience

Inside each detailed environment, the player can move through the space and see areas around them render with richer visual information. Nearby space reveals color, form, and environmental detail, while areas outside that range remain reduced to white flickering particles that suggest the broader surroundings without fully resolving them.

This creates a contrast between local clarity and distant ambiguity. Users can then find the green sound marker within the scene and use it to return to the main map, completing the loop between overview and place-specific immersion.

Process

The project began as an exploration of how personally meaningful places could be connected through a single navigable system. Rather than presenting locations as separate scenes, I wanted users to feel the relationship between overview, transition, sound, and detail.

Using Unity and custom interaction logic, I built the map layer, hover-based sound behavior, camera transition system, and spatial return loop. Gaussian splatting was then used to shape the detailed scenes so that visual fidelity changed with distance and movement, reinforcing the feeling of entering and reading space in layers.
