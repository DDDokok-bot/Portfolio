Overview

This project explores a voice-interactive software concept for cooking support. The idea is to help users manage groceries, track ingredients at home, and make cooking decisions more easily through a smart fridge voice assistant.

The system is designed to tell users what ingredients they already have, suggest recipes based on available food, recommend ingredients to buy for meals, remind users when ingredients are about to expire, and learn food preferences over time.

What I Designed

Voice Interaction Flow

Designed a conversational system that supports grocery planning, ingredient lookup, and cooking decisions through natural voice-based interaction.

Ingredient Awareness

Focused the experience on helping users remember what they already have at home and making ingredient information easier to access when planning meals.

Food Waste Support

Built the concept around practical reminders, expiration awareness, and more informed meal suggestions to reduce forgotten or wasted food.

Process

The project started with user research around how people shop for groceries, how they decide what to cook, how they remember ingredients at home, and what problems appear when food is forgotten or expires. The goal was to understand whether voice interaction could be useful in everyday kitchen situations.

Research questions focused on how people decide what groceries to buy, whether they forget what ingredients they already have, how often food expires before being used, and when voice interaction would actually be helpful. Based on those findings, I shaped a software concept centered on ingredient visibility, meal support, and timely reminders.
