Forkcast

A dish-focused restaurant discovery website that helps users find highly rated dishes nearby, see where they are served, and explore restaurant and menu details through food-specific reviews.

Overview

Forkcast rethinks restaurant discovery by shifting attention from overall restaurant ratings to specific dishes. Instead of asking users to choose a restaurant first, the website helps them discover highly rated dishes nearby and then shows where those dishes are available.

From there, users can enter a restaurant detail page, browse that restaurant's top-rated dishes, view dish-specific information, and read comments focused on individual menu items. The project emphasizes clearer decision-making for users who care more about what to eat than which restaurant to choose first.

What I Designed

Dish-First Discovery

Designed a browsing flow centered on individual dishes so users can quickly identify highly rated food items before committing to a restaurant.

Restaurant Detail Navigation

Structured restaurant pages around top dishes, dish information, and related comments to make food-specific decision-making more direct and useful.

Review Focus

Shifted the review logic from restaurant-level scoring to dish-level feedback, creating a more precise and actionable way to compare food experiences.

Process

The project began with a simple question: when users search for food, are they really searching for a restaurant, or are they searching for a specific dish they want to eat? That question shaped the main interaction model of the site.

I used HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to build a structured restaurant website that prioritizes dish discovery, dish detail views, and user comments tied to specific menu items. The result is a website concept that turns food browsing into a more targeted and decision-oriented experience.
