Overview

This project explores how simple user actions can shape the feeling of a living digital space. Instead of treating interaction as a button press or direct command, the experience is built around subtle environmental response: water movement, fish reaction, rhythm, and visual atmosphere.

The goal was to create something that feels playful and immediate, while still showing control over motion design, behavioral logic, and interactive system building.

What I Designed

Interaction System

Built a responsive interaction flow where user input affects ripple behavior, fish movement, and the overall feeling of the scene.

Motion Behavior

Designed motion rules to make the fish feel reactive rather than purely decorative, balancing predictability with liveliness.

Visual Atmosphere

Used image, motion, and timing together to create a calm but playful environment that rewards hovering, touching, and repeated exploration.

Process

The project began as an experiment in responsive motion and gradually became a study in how behavior can make a digital environment feel alive. I iterated on ripple timing, fish response, and the visual pacing of the whole scene so the interaction would feel intuitive rather than chaotic.

As the system evolved, I focused on making each action readable: when the user does something, the environment should answer in a way that feels clear, satisfying, and visually coherent.
